Scenario

Selected critical hazard

🛑

Primary hazard

The UGV receives a valid stop command, but braking and motion control do not achieve a safe stop within the required time or distance envelope.

↩️

Related manifestations

Rollback on slope, failure to hold after stop, startup movement before brake readiness, or delayed braking from sensing / arbitration issues.

⛰️

Why it matters at Bonsai

Off-road terrain, dust, mud, weather, payload shifts, and vision limitations can all reduce stopping margin for compact autonomous equipment.

System definition

Item definition & ODD assumptions

In scope

  • Service brake, hold/parking brake, propulsion inverter, wheel speed sensors, IMU, safety controller, autonomy stack, E-stop, power system, HMI.
  • Interfaces to mission control, teleoperation, diagnostics, and service tools.
  • Minimum-risk condition: controlled stop + propulsion inhibit + brake hold + controlled restart.

Operating assumptions

  • Harsh terrain: slope, loose gravel, mud, ruts, low friction, rain, dust, vibration, EMI.
  • Operational exposure: workers, equipment, trailers, fences, structures, and public-edge work areas.
  • Nominal autonomy relies heavily on perception confidence and state estimation.

Mitigation

Technical & architectural controls

Hardware / electrical

  • Brake sizing for worst-case mass, slope, and friction.
  • Hold brake or fail-safe parking strategy for stationary state integrity.
  • Independent reserve low-voltage energy to complete a stop through transients.
  • Sealed connectors, EMC robustness, vibration qualification, ingress protection.
  • Independent safety shutdown path separate from mission compute.

Software / controls

  • Safety stop command always overrides mission commands.
  • Monitor commanded decel vs measured decel and trigger minimum-risk condition before FTTI expires.
  • Plausibility across wheel speed, IMU, motor torque/current, and standstill estimate.
  • Startup interlocks, stale-data detection, heartbeat supervision, and restart lockout after safety fault.

SOTIF

Why ISO 21448 matters for Bonsai Robotics

No fault required

A vision-based UGV can behave unsafely even when hardware and software are healthy if the intended sensing function is insufficient for the scenario.

Example edge case

Mud, glare, dust, or unusual terrain causes the autonomy stack to underestimate slope or obstacle severity, so braking starts too late.

Engineering response

Define performance limits, confidence monitors, ODD restrictions, and deterministic fallback behavior when perception confidence collapses.

Validation

Validation & compliance test plan

Simulation

Slope, friction, payload, dust, occlusion, sensor bias, latency, message corruption, power faults.

SIL / HIL

Controller logic, watchdogs, bus timeouts, stale data, brownout handling, actuation feedback loops.

Prototype

Injected faults, stop distance, hold-force characterization, startup interlock checks, degraded modes.

Field

Mud, rain, night, grade, worker proximity procedures, and ODD edge-case testing.

Evidence package

Traceability from safety goal to requirement to test case; pass/fail metrics for stop distance, detection latency, hold performance, fault reaction, residual risk, and corrective-action closure.
